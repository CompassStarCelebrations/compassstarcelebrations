FAQs (Frequently Asked Questions)

How does your process work?

Your ceremony should feel personal, meaningful, and true to you. I'll guide you through a simple, comfortable process so everything feels easy from start to finish.

We'll begin with a Zoom or in-person meeting to get to know each other and talk about your ceremony and celebration.

Personalized Wedding Ceremony: a fully customized ceremony based on your story and input.

Standard Ceremony: choose from prepared options, then we confirm details together.

Simple Legal Ceremony: we focus on essential details in a straightforward planning meeting.

On your wedding day, I will guide you through the ceremony so you can simply be present and enjoy the moment.

What is the difference between your ceremony options?

The main difference is the level of personalization and planning support:

Personalized Ceremony: fully written for you and your love story.

Standard Ceremony: prepared ceremony scripts with guided planning.

Simple Legal Ceremony: a streamlined ceremony focused on legal marriage.

If you're unsure which option is right for you, I'm happy to help you decide.

Can we meet before booking?

Yes. Your initial meeting is an important part of the process. It gives us a chance to connect, talk about your ceremony, and make sure you feel comfortable moving forward.

Do you attend rehearsals?

Yes, if requested and subject to availability. Rehearsals are an additional fee.

Do you travel for weddings?

Yes! 20 km from Burlington is included (depending on package), then $0.60/km beyond that for round trip travel.

Do you do small weddings or elopements?

Yes! Standard and Simple Legal Ceremonies are perfect for intimate celebrations and elopements.

Can we write our own vows?

Yes. Vow support is included in Personalized Ceremonies (if requested), optional in Standard Ceremonies, and can be included in Simple Legal Ceremonies as well.

Do you file the marriage licence?

Yes! I mail it to the Registrar General of Ontario within 48 hours of your ceremony.

How far in advance should we book?

As early as possible, especially for peak season, but last-minute dates are always welcome if available.

Information to Know Before Getting Married in Ontario

Planning a wedding comes with lots of questions. For information about marriage licences and legal requirements in Ontario, please visit the ServiceOntario website.
